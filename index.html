Sembcorp Industries is a Singapore-listed integrated energy company in the middle of a structural transition from gas-fired generation toward a 25GW renewables target. This model was built from the ground up using the FY2025 Annual Report.

The valuation gap. Under base case assumptions — a WACC of 5.5% and a terminal growth rate of 2.0% — the model yields an implied equity value of S$9.52 per share, against a market price of S$6.24 (as of valuation date), representing an upside of approximately 52.6%. The more compelling data point is what happens under stress: even under the worst case scenario (−8% revenue in FY2026E, gross margins held flat at 22.0%, peak capex of S$1,100m), the model produces a floor value of S$7.39 — still above where the stock trades. The best case implies S$11.97.

Why the gap exists. Sembcorp is being priced, in part, for the complexity of its transition. The Alinta Energy acquisition adds 10.4GW of pipeline in Australia but includes a coal-fired asset that pushes Sembcorp's emissions intensity target from 2028 to 2035. This looks like a strategic setback on the surface — but it reflects a realistic operational compromise for a business trying to grow renewables capacity at scale while maintaining earnings stability. The JV and associate layer (S$481m in FY2026E base case, equity method) is systematically underrepresented in headline metrics — this income is stripped out and valued separately in the equity bridge, adding S$3,301m directly to intrinsic value, which standard EV/EBITDA screens tend to miss. Net debt to Adjusted EBITDA deleverages from 3.9x in FY2025A to 3.4x by FY2030E in the base case, supported by growing contracted cash flows as the renewables portfolio matures.

The asymmetry. The downside is anchored by a base of long-life, contracted utility assets with predictable cash flows — the market is pricing the transition risk heavily while undervaluing the contracted base, creating an asymmetric risk/reward profile. The upside is a call option on one of Southeast Asia's most active decarbonisation platforms.

Model Architecture

Income Statement

Revenue through Net Profit to Owners. Includes share of JV/associate income (S$496m FY2025A), D&A add-back from cost of sales, and 13.3% effective tax rate.

Balance Sheet

Full PP&E roll-forward, JV carrying value (equity method), DPN receivable wind-down, and retained earnings rollforward. BS check = 0 across all 7 years in all 3 scenarios.

Cash Flow Statement

Indirect method from PBT. Working capital movements, JV dividends received, capex, debt service, and dividend payments fully modelled.

PP&E & Depreciation Schedule

Depreciation on average PP&E — intentional circular reference resolved via iterative calculation (1,000 iterations) with a manual escape hatch on the Cover sheet.

Debt Schedule

Gross debt roll-forward, net debt, net debt/EBITDA, and interest coverage. S$100m net new borrowing p.a.; gross debt S$8,956m → S$9,456m by FY2030E (Base).

Scenario Switch

Dynamic Best/Base/Worst switch wired via INDEX/MATCH across 26 driver cells on a dedicated Inputs sheet. Single dropdown updates the entire model instantly.
