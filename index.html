3-Statement Scenario Analysis SGX: U96

Sembcorp Industries — 3-Statement Financial Model

Fully integrated income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow model covering FY2024A–FY2030E. Built from the Sembcorp FY2025 Annual Report. Features a dynamic Best / Base / Worst scenario switch across five key drivers, PP&E and debt schedules, and an interactive dashboard. Balance sheet balances to zero across all 7 years in all 3 scenarios.

Scenario
Best Case — Faster GRS price recovery, Alinta acquisition fully contributing from FY2027E, data centre PPAs accelerating demand, stronger renewable margins across India and Australia.
Base Case — Gradual GRS stabilisation, partial Alinta contribution from FY2027E, renewables mix growing toward 25GW target, progressive dividend maintained.
Worst Case — Prolonged GRS price weakness, Alinta acquisition delayed, renewables curtailment persists, management preserves cash and reduces dividend payout.

Model Architecture

Income Statement

Revenue through Net Profit to Owners. Includes share of JV/associate income (S$496m FY2025A), D&A add-back from cost of sales, and 13.3% effective tax rate.

Balance Sheet

Full PP&E roll-forward, JV carrying value (equity method), DPN receivable wind-down, and retained earnings rollforward. BS check = 0 across all 7 years in all 3 scenarios.

Cash Flow Statement

Indirect method from PBT. Working capital movements, JV dividends received, capex, debt service, and dividend payments fully modelled.

PP&E & Depreciation Schedule

Depreciation on average PP&E — intentional circular reference resolved via iterative calculation (1,000 iterations) with a manual escape hatch on the Cover sheet.

Debt Schedule

Gross debt roll-forward, net debt, net debt/EBITDA, and interest coverage. S$100m net new borrowing p.a.; gross debt S$8,956m → S$9,456m by FY2030E (Base).

Scenario Switch

Dynamic Best/Base/Worst switch wired via INDEX/MATCH across 26 driver cells on a dedicated Inputs sheet. Single dropdown updates the entire model instantly.

Key Assumptions — Base Case

Revenue −5% / −1% / +2% / +3% / +3% — GRS price headwinds in FY2026–27, stabilising as gas prices normalise and renewables capacity grows toward 25GW
Gross Margin 22.4% trough (FY2025A) recovering gradually to 23.5% by FY2029E — renewables mix shift and GRS stabilisation following Wilton 10 outage
JV Income 15.9% yield on carrying value expanding via S$100m annual investments plus retained earnings. Equity method — JV revenue not consolidated
Capex S$900m (FY2026E) stepping to S$1,000m (FY2028–30E) — continued renewable deployment below FY2024A peak of S$1,989m
Dividends 42.5% payout. One-year payment lag consistent with Sembcorp's interim/final structure — forecast dividend = prior-year DPS × shares outstanding
Debt S$100m net new borrowing p.a. Gross debt S$8,956m → S$9,456m by FY2030E. Net debt/EBITDA deleverages 4.0x → 3.4x through earnings growth
